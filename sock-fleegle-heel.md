All my socks use the [Fleegle Heel](https://fleeglesblog.blogspot.com/2007/09/no-sock-holes-for-you.html) which looks clean and does not leave bulky seems.

I knit it all on my one big loop needle toe up.

You extend the sock until the instep reaches along the bottom of your foot all the way to the end of your heel. 

Then you knit only sole stitches as follows:

1. Drop a stitch marker at the center stitch.
2. (RS) knit until 3 stitches beyond marker;  SSK  K1 turn
3. (WS) Slip1, purl until 3 stitches beyond marker on the other side;  P2tog P1 turn
4. (RS) Slip1, knit until the gap (that is the gap from your SSK-K1 in step 2); SSK (over the gap), K1 turn
5. (WS) Slip1, purl until the gap, P2tog (over the gap), P1, turn
6. Repeat descrease rows (steps 4 and 5) until the gaps are all the way on the sides.

   
Now keep on knitting in the round again to knit the leg.

I always recommend to try the sock on to make sure it fits. If the leg is too tight, then stop decrease rows earlier.


Tips

* You can work in any pattern you like. I prefer a slip stitch to give the fabric more heft:  RS: S1K1 repeat, WS; Purl all the way
* Teach yourself to knit forward and backwards without turning the work.  I knit continental "forward", but English "backwards". Its much faster and less messy on the short rows.
* Instead of `SSK; K1; turn; slip` / `P2tog; P1; turn; slip` you can also wrap the stitch then pick up and hide the wraps on the next row, but I find it more difficult to execute for no extra gains.
